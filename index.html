AI summary of the IBNR Weekly February 27, 2025

The February 27, 2025, issue of IBNR Weekly addresses several critical developments in the insurance industry:

California Homeowners' Insurance Crisis
The report highlights the escalating homeowners' insurance crisis in California, exacerbated by recent substantial wildfire losses. This situation is drawing comparisons to Florida's historically dysfunctional homeowners' market. The analysis underscores significant differences between the two states, particularly concerning post-event funding mechanisms, markets of last resort, regulatory priorities, and legislative impacts. The authors express concern over California's regulatory environment, which emphasizes consumer cost capping, potentially at the expense of insurer solvency. This stance may lead to a reduction in admitted insurance options, pushing more business into Excess and Surplus (E&S) lines and the state's FAIR Plan, which has now surpassed Florida's Citizens Property Insurance Corporation as the largest state plan, with over $450 billion in exposure. Recent reforms offer some hope for improvement, but their effectiveness remains to be seen, especially in light of the 2025 wildfires and the political nature of California's regulatory environment.

State Farm General's Financial Struggles
State Farm General (SFG) is facing significant financial challenges following the wildfires, with its surplus nearing the Authorized Control Level Risk-Based Capital (RBC) threshold. The company reports a surplus of approximately $640 million against $3.2 billion in Net Premiums Written (NPW). This precarious financial position raises concerns about SFG's ability to continue operating under current conditions and regulatory constraints.

Reserve Trends and Schedule P Analysis
The report provides an analysis of reserve trends based on initial 10-K data, indicating continued favorable development in Workers' Compensation, though less pronounced than in previous years. This positive trend is partly offset by increased attention to General Liability and Commercial Auto lines, particularly concerning recent accident years. The analysis suggests a cautious approach to these lines, given the evolving loss development patterns.

Quick Updates

  • Berkshire Hathaway's Annual Letter: The letter offers limited new information, with minimal discussion on (re)insurance operations.
  • Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA): The TWIA Board has adjusted model weightings to achieve a desired Probable Maximum Loss (PML), resulting in a 5% year-over-year decrease. This adjustment requires minimal additional reinsurance limits for the June 1 renewal.
  • Crop Insurance Market: The market is anticipating flat to slightly declining premiums for 2025.
  • Personal Auto Lead Generation: There is a broadening demand for leads across carriers and channels, indicating a competitive landscape in the personal auto insurance sector.

Earnings Reports
The issue includes earnings summaries for major insurers:

  • Munich Re: Detailed financial performance analysis.
  • Swiss Re: Insights into recent earnings and strategic initiatives.
  • AXA: Overview of financial results and market positioning.

AI summary of the IBNR Weekly February 20, 2025

As the fourth quarter earnings season for the U.S. (re)insurance sector approaches its conclusion, several key themes have emerged, reflecting both challenges and opportunities within the industry.

Earnings Season Overview
With approximately 67% of companies having reported their earnings, the sector has experienced varied performance. Underwriters have seen a slight decline of 0.6%, while brokers have enjoyed a 3.6% increase since the earnings season began on January 22, 2025. In comparison, the S&P Financials index rose by 1.3%, and the S&P 500 increased by 1.1% during the same period. Progressive stands out with an 11.1% increase, buoyed by positive January results. Conversely, companies like Selective Insurance, RenaissanceRe, American Financial, SiriusPoint, Fidelis, and Everest have underperformed, with many recognizing net adverse prior-year development (PYD) in Q4.

California Wildfire Losses
The California wildfires have had a significant impact, with current loss estimates around $20 billion and projections exceeding $40 billion. In response, the California Department of Insurance has requested State Farm to justify its proposed rate increase, indicating heightened regulatory scrutiny.

Russia/Ukraine Aviation Litigation
Geopolitical tensions have also influenced the sector. Fidelis Insurance reported a net charge of $287 million due to aviation litigation related to the Russia/Ukraine conflict, highlighting the broader implications of international events on the insurance industry.

Commercial Insurance Pricing Trends
The Council of Insurance Agents & Brokers (CIAB) Market Index moderated to a 5.4% increase in Q4, down from 5.7% in Q3. This suggests a slight easing in commercial insurance pricing trends, which could influence underwriting strategies moving forward.

Personal Auto Insurance Market
In the personal auto insurance sector, elevated shopping activity persisted through year-end, as reported by LexisNexis. This trend indicates ongoing competition and consumer engagement, factors that insurers must navigate to maintain market share.

Total Value Creation (TVC) Scorecard
The 2024 Dowling & Partners (D&P) Composite Simple Average for Total Value Creation at Cost was +19%, with individual company performance ranging from over +40% for Palomar Holdings and Progressive to -25% for James River Group Holdings. The two-year forward TVC at cost is approximately 14%, compared to approximately 11% over the last three years, suggesting a cautiously optimistic outlook for the sector.

Additional Observations
The report also provides a detailed composite price performance analysis since previous market highs on February 19, 2020, offering insights into individual company performances over this period. As the earnings season concludes, attention is shifting toward upcoming 10-K filings and statutory data releases, which will offer a more comprehensive view of reserve trends and market share dynamics.
