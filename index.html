IBNR Weekly Insurance Industry Update

Published on February 20, 2025

The Q4 U.S. insurance earnings season is nearly complete on a market cap basis, with 19 companies still to report. Major themes include casualty loss trends, California fire loss exposure, commercial lines pricing, and personal auto competition. Since earnings began on January 22, the underwriter composite is down 0.6% while brokers are up 3.6%.

IBNR Weekly Podcast

Key highlights:

  • California wildfire industry losses have reached approximately $20 billion, with potential to exceed $40 billion
  • Notable company losses include Farmers ($850M), Zurich ($200M), Fidelis ($188-224M), and Conduit Re ($100-140M)
  • State Farm is facing regulatory scrutiny over proposed rate increases in California (22% for homeowners, 15% for renters)
  • Specialty reinsurer Fidelis announced a $287M net charge related to Russia/Ukraine aviation litigation
  • Commercial insurance rate increases have moderated to +5.4% in Q4 (from +5.7% in Q3)
  • Personal auto shopping remains elevated with 45% of policies-in-force shopped at least once in 2024

The industry faces several critical issues for 2025, including the hardening property market in California, accelerating casualty trends, and reinsurers' evolving approach to wildfire risk. Most carriers maintain strong capital positions despite recent catastrophe events, with increased share repurchase activity expected throughout the year.

Dowling IBNR Weekly Summary: February 24, 2025

Published on February 26, 2025

This week's Dowling report highlights significant developments in the reinsurance market, with property cat rates showing signs of stabilization after two years of hardening. The report also notes increased interest from capital markets in insurance-linked securities (ILS), potentially indicating a shift in market dynamics.

Key points:

  • Property cat rate increases slowing to single digits
  • New capital entering the ILS market
  • Technology investment in cat modeling showing promising results

Our analysis: The cooling rate environment presents both challenges and opportunities for insurtech companies focused on property risk. We're seeing potential for partnerships between traditional carriers and AI-powered startups that can provide more granular risk assessment.

Specialty Insurance Market Update: Cyber Trends

Published on February 22, 2025

The cyber insurance market continues to evolve rapidly with claims frequency increasing while severity has somewhat stabilized. New AI-powered security tools are beginning to impact underwriting models, with carriers increasingly differentiating based on adoption of these technologies.

We're closely monitoring how large language models are being deployed for both attack and defense in the cybersecurity landscape, with implications for coverage terms and pricing models.
